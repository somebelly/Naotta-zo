A world is a 3-tuple $(\Omega,A,\delta)$ such that:
  • $\Omega,A$ are two nonempty sets;
  • $\delta:\Omega\times A^2\to\GF(2)=\{0,1\}$.
An element of $\Omega$ is called a state. An element of $A$ is called a thing.

In the rest of this daydream, $(\Omega,A,\delta)$ is a world as defined above.

Let $\omega\in\Omega$ and $a\in A$. For $b\in A$, we say thing $a$ thinks thing $b$ exists in state $\omega$ if $\delta(\omega,a,b)=1$, and the opposite if $\delta(\omega,a,b)=0$. The subjective world of $a$ in state $\omega$ is defined as $\SW_{a,\omega}:=\big(\delta(\omega,a,b)\big)_{b\in A}\in \GF(2)^A$.

For $x\in\{0,1\}$, we note:

  • for $\omega\in\Omega$ and $a\in A$, $\[x\]_{a,\omega}:=\{b\in A\mid \delta(\omega,a,b)=x\}$;
  • for $a\in A$, $\[x\]_{a}:=\{b\in A\mid\forall\omega\in\Omega,\, \delta(\omega,a,b)=x\}$;
  • $\[x\]:=\{b\in A\mid\forall\omega\in\Omega,\,\forall a\in A,\, \delta(\omega,a,b)=x\}$.

We say $a\in A$ is a living thing if $a$ always thinks itself existing, i.e. $a\in\[1\]_{a}$.

For $a,b\in A$, we say $a$ senses $b$ (noted $a\S b$) if $\exists \omega\in\Omega$ such that:
  • $b\in\[1\]_{a,\omega}$, and that
  • $\forall x\in\[1\]_{a,\omega}$, $b\in\[1\]_{x,\omega}$.
